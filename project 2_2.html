Introduction¶

This no-show appointment dataset collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointments. A number of characteristics about the patient are included in each row. In this report, we are trying to investigate the important factors associated with the show up rate of appointments. Therefore, we are going to answer the following questions:

  1. Is gender of a patient affecting his/her show up rate of appointments
  2. Is age of a patient affecting his/her show up rate of appointments
  3. Is the show up rate affected by whether the patient is enrolled in Brasilian welfare program?
  4. Is the show up rate affected by whether the patient is an alcoholic?
  5. Is the show up rate affected by whether the appointment is on weekend or weekdays, or any specific day of a week?
  6. Is the show up rate affected by whether a message is received?
  7. Is the show up rate affected by whether the patients are disabled?
  8. Is the show up rate affected by whether the scheduled day is same or different with the appointment day?

By answering these questions, we will have a better understanding of the dataset.

Conclusions¶

From the dataset, we can see:

  1. Male patients have higher show up rate to their appointments;
  2. Altough the middle-age patients made more appointments than other two age gourps, the elder people have higher show up rate to their appointments;
  3. People who are not enrolled in Brasilian welfare program have higher show up rate to their appointments;
  4. Whether a patient is an alcoholic does not affect his/her show up rate;
  5. Whether the appointment is on weekend or weekdays does not affect the show up rate, however, appointments on Thursdays and Fridays have lower show up rates of the patients;
  6. If the appointment is scheduled on the same day with the appointment day, it can result in higher show up rate;
  7. Disabled patients have higher show-up rate;
  8. Receiving messages results in lower show up rate;.

Limitations:

  1. The location of hospital is a potential important factor to affect the show up rate for appointments, however, due to the lack of the knowledge of locations, it is hard to investigate the effect of this factor;
  2. The 100k medication records in the dataset are from about 48k patients, which means some of the information is repeated. It would be better if there is some way to exclude the effects from same patients.
